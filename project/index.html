Why Adepto?

In the classroom we can classify assessments into two main categories. There are formative assessments and summative assessments. Summative assessments take the form of exams, testing, final projects or papers. Summative assessments are easily recognized because they are usually given a grade. Formative assessments are recognizable because they are not given a grade and are used as assessments for learning. Formative assessments allow an instructor to gauge the ability of the student, but the results are used to modify future lessons and curriculum (O’Malley and Pierce 1996).

This assessment tool allows for continuous assessment for instruction. Assess students each day on their areas of struggle and track their progress. Use the tool as an exit ticket on their way out the door or while waiting in the hallways.

O’Malley, J. M. & Valdez Pierce, L. (1996). Authentic assessment for English language learners: Practical approaches for teachers. New York: Pearson Longman.
